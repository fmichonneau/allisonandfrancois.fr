Accommodations

If you are from out of town and need accommodations, we suggest you stay at one of the Bed and Breakfasts situated close to downtown. There are many hotels in town. The Hampton Inn is conveniently located half-way between where the ceremony and the reception will be held. Airbnb is also an option.

Restaurants

Gainesville offers a good selection of restaurants. Dragonfly is a Japanese restaurant with excellent sushi and small plates. Crane makes delicious ramen, and is one of our favorite places. If you are looking for pizzas, Satchel’s is a Gainesville institution, V pizza makes good Italian-style pizzas, and Big Lou’s makes New York style ones. The Top serves New American food and has a large selection of drinks to accompany your dinner. For a special dinner in an intimate atmosphere Manuel’s Vintage Room makes good pasta and steaks.

For lunch, the Vine Bakery and Daily Green (good vegetarian/vegan options) are good places to check out.

For quick and authentic Mexican food, El Indio and La Tienda are excellent choices.

If you like to check out local breweries, First Magnitude and Swamp Head offer a large variety of beers.

Things to do

The natural areas around Gainesville are spectacular. Just outside of town, Payne’s Prairie (check out the La Chua Trail) and Sweetwater Park give you the opportunity to see alligators and many bird species. A little further away (45 min), you can kayak down the gentle flow of the Ichetuknee Springs State Park. At Devil’s Millhopper you can walk down into a sink hole and learn about the geology of Florida.

At the Florida Museum, you will learn about the natural history of Florida, and the culture of the native people. Inside the museum, you can walk around and observe live butterflies at the Butterfly Rainforest. Next door, the Harn Museum of Art is known for its African, Asian, Contemporary and Photography Collections.

The Kanapaha Gardens displays a wide variety of native and exotic plants in a beautiful and relaxing setting.

There is a bike sharing service in town, that you can use to explore part (or all) of the Hawthorne trail, or just take a stroll around Depot Park.
